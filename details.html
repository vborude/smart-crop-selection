Cotton

▼
Type: Cash Crop
Soil: Black soil
Season: Kharif
Temp: 21–30°C
Rainfall: 50–100 cm
Yield: 15–20 q/ha

Type: Cash Crop
Soil: Deep black (Regur), well-drained
Season: Kharif
Temperature: 21–30°C
Rainfall: 50–100 cm
Sowing: June–July
Harvest: Oct–Jan
Yield: 15–20 q/ha

Overview:
Cotton is one of the most important commercial crops used in the textile industry. It requires a long frost-free period, bright sunshine, and moderate rainfall. Black soil helps retain moisture which supports uniform plant growth.

Growth Requirements:
• Requires warm climate and high sunlight
• Needs well-drained soil to avoid root damage
• Moderate irrigation is necessary during flowering stage

Uses:
• Textile fiber production
• Cottonseed oil
• Industrial raw material

Advantages:
• High market demand
• Suitable for large-scale farming

Precautions:
• Avoid waterlogging
• Protect from bollworm and pests
• Use drip irrigation for better efficiency.

Wheat

▼
Type: Food Crop
Season: Rabi
Temp: 10–25°C
Type: Food Crop
Soil: Fertile loamy soil
Season: Rabi
Temperature: 10–25°C
Sowing: Oct–Nov
Harvest: Mar–Apr
Yield: 25–30 q/ha

Overview:
Wheat is a staple food crop widely consumed in India. It requires cool climate during growth and warm conditions during harvesting. It is used to produce flour for bread and chapati.

Growth Requirements:
• Cool temperature during early stages
• Moderate irrigation required
• Needs fertile soil for high yield

Uses:
• Flour production
• Bakery products

Advantages:
• High productivity
• Essential food crop

Precautions:
• Avoid excessive irrigation
• Protect from fungal diseases like rust

Sugarcane

▼
Water: High
Used for sugar production.
Type: Cash Crop
Soil: Deep fertile loamy soil (well-drained)
Season: Mainly Kharif (can be grown year-round in some regions)
Temperature: 20–35°C
Rainfall: 75–150 cm
Sowing: Jan–March (Spring) or June–July
Harvest: 10–18 months after sowing
Water Requirement: Very High
Yield: 60–100 tons/hectare

Overview:
Sugarcane is a major commercial crop used for sugar production. It is a long-duration crop that requires a tropical to subtropical climate, abundant sunlight, and continuous water supply. It plays a vital role in the sugar and ethanol industry.

Growth Requirements:
• Requires fertile soil rich in organic matter
• Needs regular irrigation throughout growth
• Performs best in warm and humid climate
• Requires proper spacing for healthy cane development

Uses:
• Sugar production
• Jaggery (Gur) and molasses
• Ethanol and biofuel production
• By-products used in paper and bio-energy industries

Advantages:
• High economic value crop
• Supports multiple industries
• Provides employment in rural areas

Precautions:
• Avoid water stress during growth stages
• Protect from pests like borers and diseases like red rot
• Ensure proper drainage to prevent root damage

Soybean

▼
Type: Oilseed Crop
Soil: Well-drained loamy or black soil
Season: Kharif
Temperature: 20–30°C
Rainfall: 60–100 cm
Sowing: June–July
Harvest: October–November
Water Requirement: Moderate
Yield: 20–25 q/ha

Overview:
Soybean is an important oilseed and protein-rich crop. It is widely grown for edible oil production and is also used in various food and industrial products. It improves soil fertility by fixing nitrogen in the soil.

Growth Requirements:
• Requires moderate rainfall and warm climate
• Needs well-drained soil to prevent root rot
• Sensitive to waterlogging
• Requires proper spacing and weed control

Uses:
• Edible oil production
• Animal feed (soy meal)
• Food products like soy milk, tofu
• Industrial uses (paint, biodiesel)

Advantages:
• High protein content
• Improves soil fertility (nitrogen fixation)
• Good market demand

Precautions:
• Avoid excess water or flooding
• Control weeds in early growth stage
• Protect from pests like aphids and diseases

Mustard

▼
Type: Oilseed Crop
Soil: Well-drained loamy to alluvial soil
Season: Rabi
Temperature: 10–25°C
Rainfall: 25–40 cm
Sowing: October–November
Harvest: February–March
Water Requirement: Low to Moderate
Yield: 10–15 q/ha

Overview:
Mustard is an important oilseed crop widely grown in India for edible oil production. It grows best in cool climates and requires relatively less water compared to other crops. It is a short-duration crop and fits well in crop rotation systems.

Growth Requirements:
• Requires cool climate during early growth stages
• Needs well-drained soil to avoid root damage
• Grows well with limited irrigation
• Requires proper sunlight for better seed formation

Uses:
• Mustard oil (cooking oil)
• Used in pickles and spices
• Oil cake used as cattle feed
• Medicinal and industrial uses

Advantages:
• Low water requirement
• Short duration crop (quick harvest)
• Good profitability with low input cost
• Suitable for crop rotation

Precautions:
• Avoid waterlogging conditions
• Protect from pests like aphids
• Proper weed control in early stage
• Timely harvesting to prevent seed loss
